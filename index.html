01

Data. Differentiation. Discretion.

The one thing worth building first, and protecting from the start.

ORVÉA Private's value is its knowledge of each client: their family, their home, and how they see their life perfected. That knowledge is hard to earn and harder still to replicate once gained. It is what sets ORVÉA Private apart, and it is what the technology should be built around.

This knowledge is specific, accumulated and extremely private: how to enter each home, how the owner likes it kept, what to stock, what has been done and when, and what "ready" means for this particular family. A competitor can build a similar app in a month. They cannot build this. It is earned one home and one arrival at a time, and it deepens with every visit. Because of what it holds, and the clients it belongs to, protecting it is not a later consideration. It is factored in from the start.

Off-the-shelf platforms do not hold knowledge this way. A CRM, a task tool and a notes app each keep a fragment, and the full picture lives nowhere. The original proposal was built on those tools. It would work, but it would silo the very thing that sets ORVÉA Private apart, and make it harder to use as the business grows.

Built properly, the knowledge lives in one structured record that can evolve into any future system without losing what makes it valuable. Built poorly, it constrains what can be done, and every change takes longer and costs more.

This is why the recommended approach begins with the knowledge itself, and treats the apps and portals as things built on top of it, in time.

02

The recommended approach: four stages

Each stage answers one question before the next begins.

The approach builds in four stages. Each one answers a single question before the next begins, so money is only ever committed to something already proven.

0

Discover and classify

What must we know?

Before any tool is chosen, map what the service actually requires. Simulate what a client may ask, and what the operator must do to deliver it. Capture, by the simplest means, what information each step needs, and classify each piece for sensitivity, from routine detail to anything that could reveal a home or when it sits empty.

Founder time only
1

Shape the schema

Can we structure it to last?

Turn that picture into a structured, flexible record, built on foundations that can grow rather than a shape borrowed from another company's product. Done quickly and cheaply in a tool like Airtable, where the founder can build, test and feel the structure on their own homes. No external parties, no client data at risk. The value is not the tool. It is the proven schema, portable to whatever is built next.

Tens of dollars a month
2

Prove the process, and harden it

Does the process work, safely?

Add a simple working interface, not the finished experience, but enough to confirm the whole process runs end to end. This is the first time anyone outside ORVÉA Private touches the system, so the proven schema moves onto a foundation built for real security. A capable database such as Supabase enforces, in the data itself, that a vendor sees only their own job, never a client's identity or a home's occupancy.

Low hundreds a month
3

Build the experience

Does it meet the brand standard?

With the record proven and the process working, build the experience to the brand standard. The client-facing experience takes priority, with operator and vendor surfaces built to match, so everything carrying the ORVÉA Private name feels considered. Security is engineered to its full standard here. Because the Stage 2 foundation is already the right one, this is a refinement, not a rebuild.

Deferring the polished experience is sequencing, not sacrifice. Because the record is structured from day one, any experience can sit on top of it when it is wanted.

03

What it costs

Not necessarily less in total. Committed last, not first.

This approach does not necessarily reduce the total cost of reaching a finished platform. What it changes is when the money is required. Almost all meaningful spend is held back until the model is proven, so very little capital is at risk before there is any evidence the business works.

The early stages are inexpensive on purpose. Their job is to prove the differentiating asset, the schema, and to set the security foundation correctly, before a single dollar is committed to building an experience. The money follows the same two principles as the rest of this recommendation. Spend first on what differentiates, and on what protects.

The original proposal commits most of its cost at the start, before the model is validated. This approach commits it last.

04

Security

Classify early, control progressively.

Discretion is the product, so security is not a final stage. It is decided at the start and built up as exposure grows.

The decision is made early. In Stage 0, every piece of information is classified by how damaging its exposure would be, the most sensitive being anything that reveals a home's security, or when it sits empty. That judgement is made before any tool is chosen, and it does not change.

The controls grow with exposure. While only the founder uses the system, light controls are enough. The first real test comes at Stage 2, when a vendor is given access and must see only their assigned job, a genuine boundary enforced in the data itself, not a field hidden on a screen. That is why the schema moves onto a proper database at this point. The full standard a security-conscious client expects is engineered at Stage 3.
